Korean Food

Korean Army Stew

  • Instant noodles, tteok, American cheese, sausages, Spam, and various veggies make up this jumbled stew that goes back to the immediate aftermath of the Korean War.
  • Since meat was in short supply, chefs came up with inventive ways to replace it using the excess food from the American army installation stationed in Seoul, thus the stew's name.
  • A buddae jjigae without Spam is unthinkable, despite the fact that meat has now become widely available.

    • Tteokbokki

  • The famous red-orange rice cakes (tteokbokki), which are steamed and cut into slices and served with fish cakes (oden) and scallions in a sweet and spicy sauce made of chili paste, are so well-liked that a entire part of Seoul is dedicated to them.
  • The sauce has been reported to contain a variety of ingredients, including ketchup and black soybean paste. The more pepper the better. Tteokbokki can be found in Korean army stews as well.

    • Kimchi

  • Last but definitely not least, kimchi. Although the ingredients of kimchi might vary, they often consist of a mixture of veggies, garlic, ginger, chili peppers, salt, and fish sauce. Originally, the mixture was pickled and fermented as a means to store the veggies for the winter. The most popular vegetable used to create kimchi is cabbage, although other typical ingredients include carrots, radish, cucumber, and scallions. It's quite simple to turn kimchi into a vegan dish by using only plant-based components. There are hundreds of kimchi recipes that differ depending on the place and season in which they are made. Kimchi is the most common dish served and seen in restaurants and homes.

    Indian Food

    Briyani

  • A combined rice dish known as biryani first appeared among the royal khansamas of the durbar of Old Delhi during the Mughal Empire's late 16th century reign.
  • It is prepared with rice, Indian spices, and typically some form of meat (chicken, beef, goat, lamb, shrimp, fish), however it can also be made without any meat and occasionally with the addition of eggs and potatoes.
  • One of the most well-liked foods in South Asia and among the diaspora from the region is biryani. Similar meals are also made in various countries of the world, including Iraq, Malaysia, Thailand, Singapore, and the Arabian Peninsula.
  • On Indian online meal ordering and delivery services, biryani is the only dish that receives the most orders.

    • Indian Breads

  • Indian breads are a wide variety of flatbreads and crêpes which are an integral part of Indian cuisine.
  • Their variation reflects the diversity of Indian culture and food habits
  • Different varieties of Indian bread and pancake include Chapati, Phulka, Puri, Roti, Bajra Rotla, Thepla, Paratha, Naan, Kulcha, Bhatoora, Appam, Dosa, Luchi, Puran Poli, Pathiri, Parotta and many more. Some of these, like Paratha and Roti have many varieties. Some varieties depend on the kind of grain used to prepare them, and others depend on the fillings they contain.

    • Indian Sweets

  • Indians are known for their unique taste and experimental behavior when it comes to food. Many Indian desserts are fried foods made with sugar, milk or condensed milk. Ingredients and preferred types of dessert vary by region.
  • For instance, the majority are based on milk products in the eastern region of India. Many have almond and pistachio flavors, are flavored with cardamom, nutmeg, cloves, and black pepper, and include nuts or gold or silver leaf as decorations.

    Chinese Food

    Fried Rice

  • Fried Rice ingredients – raw rice, meat stock, seasoning, cooking oil and mixed veggies
  • Invention of fried rice – It was the prevention of waste food therefore using up leftovers
  • Origin – Fried rice was first developed during the Sui dynasty in China
  • Fun fact – it is one of the world’s most popular rice dishes as it is very adaptable especially when you’re cleaning out the refrigerator and want to get rid of the leftovers. Eg: meat and vegetables

    • Soup Dumplings

  • Soup dumplings ingredients – dough, soup (pork bones, foot, scallions, ginger and wine) , mix filling ( ground pork, garlic, soy sauce, salt, wine, sugar, oil, ginger and pepper)
  • Invention of soup dumplings – A guy named, Huang Mingxian wanted to create a dumpling that would surprise and delight the guests in his restaurant
  • Origin– It was invented by a man name Huang, the owner of a restaurant called ri Hua Xuan in Nanxiang, a district in China
  • Fun fact – the name of this popular Chinese dish means “ little basket bun”

    • Hainanese Chicken Rice

  • Hainanese Chicken rice ingredients – poached white chicken, fragrant rice, light soy sauce
  • Invention of Hainanese chicken rice – this dish has been modified over time with local influences from China
  • Origin – It was originally on an island on the South coast of China, Wencheng district of Hainan
  • Fun fact – It is the only type of chicken rice found in Haina, an island off the southern coast of China

    Malay food

    Nasi Lemak

  • Nasi lemak is a rice dish commonly served with roasted nuts, egg, ikan bilis (anchovies), and slices of cucumber.
  • Its distinctive taste comes from cooking the rice in coconut milk and pandan leaves which gives the dish its rich flavour and fragrant aroma. It is rounded off with spicy sambal – a chilli paste made from dried chilies, garlic, shallots, and belacan (shrimp paste).

    • Mee Siam

  • Mee siam is a dish of bee hoon (rice vermicelli) with a unique sweet and tart gravy.
  • The sweet and sour gravy of mee siam is reminiscent of the flavours of Thailand.
  • The gravy, made with tamarind, sugar, shrimp, belacan (shrimp paste) and soya bean paste, is poured over the bee hoon and the dish topped with sliced hard boiled egg, fried tau pok (bean curd), bean sprouts and Chinese chives.
  • A dollop of sambal tumis (a type of chilli paste) rounds off the dish.

    • Lontong

  • Lontong refers to compressed rice cakes, usually served as an accompaniment to curries and stews or as a side dish with peanut-based sauces.
  • In Singapore, the term lontong also refers to a dish of vegetable stew in coconut curry, known as sayur lodeh, to which the compressed rice is added.
  • Traditionally, lontong is compressed rice wrapped in banana leaves shaped like a cylinder.
  • The rice is infused with the aroma from the banana leaves, and also given a slightly green tint.
